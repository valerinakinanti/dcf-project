View
Modestly undervalued on a base-case DCF (+3.6%), more meaningfully so if the market re-rates toward its own current EV/EBITDA multiple (+15.6%). The spread between the two methods implies the market's current multiple already prices in some EBITDA recovery beyond a pure perpetuity-growth base case. Valuation is most sensitive to WACC — a 1% move swings the implied price by roughly $25-30. Full risk discussion in investment_memo.md.
FCFF = NOPAT + D&A − Capex − ΔNWC, discounted at WACC. Base case deliberately normalizes through the EIA's projected near-term Brent price spike (~$105/bbl, Strait of Hormuz disruption) rather than extrapolating it — that risk is captured in sensitivity, not the base case.
