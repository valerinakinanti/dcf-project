**View:** Modestly undervalued on a base-case DCF, more meaningfully undervalued
if the market re-rates toward its own current EV/EBITDA multiple on a
normalized 2030 EBITDA base. The spread between the two methods (Gordon
Growth vs. exit multiple) is itself informative — it implies the market's
current multiple is pricing in some EBITDA recovery beyond what a pure
perpetuity-growth assumption captures.

## Key Assumptions
- **WACC: 7.42%** — Cost of equity 7.80% (CAPM: risk-free 4.5% + beta 0.66 x
  ERP 5.0%), after-tax cost of debt 4.35%, weighted 88.9% equity / 11.1% debt
  by market value.
- **Revenue:** normalized growth of 2.0-3.0% p.a. off a 2025 base of $189.0B
  — deliberately NOT chasing the near-term Brent price spike to ~$105/bbl
  driven by the Strait of Hormuz disruption (EIA STEO, mid-2026), which the
  EIA itself expects to normalize to ~$79/bbl by 2027. That near-term
  volatility is captured in sensitivity, not the base case.
- **EBITDA margin:** 22.0-22.5%, roughly in line with the 2025 realized
  margin (21.9%).
- **Terminal growth:** 2.5%, consistent with long-run nominal GDP growth —
  standard for a mature, ex-growth integrated major.

**Read:** the current market price ($202.50) is bracketed within a realistic
WACC/terminal-growth range — i.e., the market isn't pricing in either
extreme optimism or pessimism. The valuation is most sensitive to WACC (a
1% move in WACC swings the implied price by roughly $25-30), which is the
correct thing to stress-test first in any DCF defense.

## Risks to the Thesis
1. **Oil price normalization risk:** if Brent falls faster than the EIA's
   ~$79/bbl 2027 base case (e.g., resolution of the Strait of Hormuz
   disruption sooner than expected), 2026-2027 EBITDA margins could compress
   below the 22% assumption used here.
2. **Multiple compression:** the exit-multiple method assumes the market
   continues to award CVX today's 11.5x EV/EBITDA — a multiple that is
   itself elevated versus the sector's historical 5-6x range, partly because
   current EBITDA is cyclically depressed. If EBITDA recovers without
   multiple compression, the exit-multiple value is achievable; if the
   multiple reverts toward historical norms as EBITDA recovers, actual
   upside would land closer to the Gordon Growth estimate.
3. **Capital allocation:** capex assumptions (9.0% of revenue) assume
   continuity of current capital discipline; a shift toward higher
   growth capex would reduce near-term FCFF.
